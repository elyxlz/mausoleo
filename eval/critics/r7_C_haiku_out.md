So I came to this project from the engineering side—spent first year building retrieval-augmented generation pipelines for technical docs, and wanted to do something final-year that'd test the same techniques against material that doesn't chunk nicely. Italian regime newspapers seemed like the obvious choice given I actually read Italian. *Il Messaggero* 1943 had the most complete scans available, which is why I ended up with a corpus missing 26 July. Two weeks went into a post-correction pass that somehow made the composite OCR scores worse, and another week figuring out that the date-bounded queries the system needed to answer best were exactly what a keyword baseline structurally couldn't touch. I'd read Bartlett's *Remembering* back in second-year psychology and dug it back up once the OCR work started spitting out day-summary nodes; the hippocampal-mapping stuff in Eichenbaum and Whittington showed up much later, after I'd already built the calendar-shaped index.

Dr Yi Gong supervised the whole thing. Her comments on the cognitive-science framing—what the system can actually warrant and what it can't—shaped pretty much everything that follows.
