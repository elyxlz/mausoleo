What unites these systems is the access template: users submit a query, obtain ranked articles, optionally filter by date or source, and examine results individually. Düring et al. (2024) characterise Impresso's interface as one of "transparent generosity" about exposing intermediate structure, yet assume the user arrives with a pre-formed search term. This presupposition works well for traditional historical research, where scholars begin with defined questions. The model falters when a historian seeks to understand a corpus too large for article-level reading. Equally problematic is research where the answer cannot take the form of an article list; when what matters is the temporal shape of coverage across days, or perhaps a striking absence rather than documented presence.
